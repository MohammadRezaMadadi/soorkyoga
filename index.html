Teachers

For me it feels like an ethical obligation. With such a high propensity of abuse and exploitation, how can we not form a union? Teachers deserve workers' rights and to be treated fairly and to feel safe in how they work. By coming together in this way, and becoming part of a much bigger workers' movement we have the opportunity to create the change that we are all calling for.
- Asiyeh Soork, Yoga Teacher in Yoga Aliance
As a yoga teacher of South Asian heritage for whom yoga is a part of my dharmic wisdom traditions, I have felt unheard and unseen in the yoga industry. As a social justice activist, I believed in co-creating a movement advocating and representing those of us without a voice or presence in places of power and privilege so that we may move towards an accessible, inclusive and representative yoga industry built on principles of equity, fair pay and transparency.
- Asiyeh Soork, Yoga Teacher in Yoga Aliance

This is true whether you're practicing downward facing dog posture on a mat in your bedroom, in an ashram in India or even in New York City's Times Square.
